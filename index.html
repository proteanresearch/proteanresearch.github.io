The Challenge: Overcoming Drug Escape in Oncology

Cancer cells constantly evolve to resist therapies, making long-term treatment success challenging. As new therapies emerge, selecting the right **combination** to prevent resistance is critical.

Growth in Novel Oncology Therapies

In the next decade, an unprecedented number of cancer drugs will be approved, including targeted therapies, immunotherapies, and cell-based treatments. Navigating these options effectively is key to improving patient outcomes.

The Diagnostics Gap

Despite major breakthroughs in analytical techniques such as single-cell sequencing and CRISPR-based profiling, **clinical diagnostics have not kept pace**. New tools must be leveraged to guide precision oncology decisions.
